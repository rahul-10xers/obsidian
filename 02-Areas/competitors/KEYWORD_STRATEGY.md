## TL;DR — The Opportunity in One Paragraph

CFBR (Commenting For Better Reach) is LinkedIn's native algorithm mechanic — and GrowReach **is** the CFBR product. Competitors rank for the term with blog posts only (TryKondo pos 4-16, Buffer, Octopus CRM, LinkedHelper) but none have the product. GrowReach should own the CFBR cluster completely. Beyond CFBR, the "linkedin comment" keyword family is wide open: neither Waalaxy nor Expandi target it (different ICP), and none of the current SERP winners (Logicballs, Planable, PhantomBuster) are direct competing SaaS products. No competitor bids on these keywords in Google Ads — confirmed blue ocean.

## AI Search / AI Overview Opportunity

From the SemRush AI search study (March 2026 data from agent research):
- LinkedIn ranks #2 in AI citations (11% average across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Mode)
- Median cited posts: 15-25 reactions, **≤1 comment** — engagement volume ≠ AI ranking
- Content sweet spots: 50-299 words OR 500-2,000 words, educational, original

**Implication for GrowReach:** Blog posts optimized for AI Overview citations should be educational, original, and cite statistics. The CFBR definition article is a prime AI Overview candidate — nobody has written the definitive version yet.

**AI Overview targets (zero-click value + brand awareness):**
- "What is CFBR LinkedIn?" → definitional, short, ideal for AI snippets
- "Is LinkedIn comment automation safe?" → FAQ format, high trust signal
- "How does LinkedIn algorithm work 2026?" → educational, citable
